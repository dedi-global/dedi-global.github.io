Three Critical Trust Gaps DeDi Solves

Every day, organizations struggle with verifying critical public information. DeDi provides the universal infrastructure for cryptographic trust.

Public Key Registries

The Problem: Public key certificates are buried in websites, rotated frequently, and require manual discovery. There's no seamless way to automatically verify authenticity.

DeDi Solution: A universal, always-current registry where public keys are instantly discoverable and cryptographically verifiable. No more manual exchanges or trust assumptions.

// Before DeDi
Manual search → Download → Verify → Hope it's current
// With DeDi
Automatic discovery → Instant verification → Always current

Global Trust Chain Example:

International Bank trusts Home Central Bank
Home Central Bank recognizes Foreign Central Bank
Foreign Central Bank approves Local Bank → International Bank trusts Local Bank

Membership Lists

The Problem: Regulators publish member lists (approved banks, certified manufacturers, accredited schools) but there's no standardized way to consume or verify this information.

DeDi Solution: Create chains of trust where organizations can recognize each other's registries, building a global web of verifiable memberships.

Revocation Lists

The Problem: Credentials expire, licenses get revoked, ratings downgrade - but these critical updates are scattered across disconnected systems.

DeDi Solution: Real-time revocation propagation across all connected systems. When a credential is revoked, everyone knows instantly.

Trade License #12345 Revoked
Credit Rating AAA Downgraded
Updates propagate instantly
